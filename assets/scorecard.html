Perimeter & Edge Security · ranked by CISA KEV + disclosure, not marketing

Raw CVE counts mislead.

Install-base size inflates a vendor's CVE/KEV total and the research attention it draws — a confounder, not a verdict. These six are ranked by the signals market share can't fake: time-to-mass-exploitation, per-unit KEV density, and disclosure transparency.

Its own cloud-backup service was breached and turned against customers. Stolen configs fed downstream ransomware; breach scope was walked back from “<5%” to all backup customers.

IvantiConnect Secure SSL-VPN
Chained zero-days exploited before any patch existed (ED 24-01 ordered federal disconnect) — then a critical RCE recurrence a year later.

~8 days from advisory to mass exploitation on the 2023 J-Web chain; nation-state implants later found on carrier-grade routers.

CiscoASA / FTD
One state actor burned four ASA/FTD zero-days across two ArcaneDoor campaigns. Its high raw KEV count is all-product scale, not firewalls alone.

Four pre-auth SSL-VPN KEV bugs in one daemon over five years (three RCE); documented silent patching — counter to the “most transparent” claim.
Rank = demonstrated incident risk + disclosure posture within a bounded recent window — not raw CVE count, and not total product quality. Lower rank ≠ “safe.” Every claim is sourced in the repo.
